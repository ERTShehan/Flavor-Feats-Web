Chicken Curry

Sri Lankan Chicken Curry is a culinary masterpiece that embodies the essence of Sri Lankan cuisine—bold, aromatic, and deeply flavorful. This dish is a celebration of spices, coconut, and tender chicken, simmered to perfection in a rich, fragrant gravy. It’s a dish that warms the soul and tantalizes the taste buds, making it a favorite in households and festive occasions across Sri Lanka.

What Makes Sri Lankan Chicken Curry Unique?

  1. Layered Spices: The curry is built on a foundation of whole spices like cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves, which are toasted to release their essential oils. These are complemented by ground spices like turmeric, chili powder, and Sri Lankan roasted curry powder, creating a complex flavor profile.
  2. Coconut Milk: The use of coconut milk adds a creamy, luxurious texture and a subtle sweetness that balances the heat from the spices.
  3. Curry Leaves: Fresh curry leaves are a signature ingredient in Sri Lankan cooking. They add a distinct, earthy aroma that is unmistakable.
  4. Slow Cooking: The chicken is simmered slowly, allowing it to absorb all the flavors of the spices and coconut milk, resulting in tender, juicy meat that falls off the bone.

Ingredients:

For the Curry:

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces, bone-in for more flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons oil (coconut oil or vegetable oil)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 sprig curry leaves (about 10-12 leaves)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (about 2 inches)
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons Sri Lankan roasted curry powder (or regular curry powder)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1 cup thin coconut milk (or water)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lime or lemon juice (optional, for tanginess)

For the Spice Paste (Optional but Recommended):

  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2-3 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Spice Paste (Optional)
  1. Dry roast the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, black peppercorns, and dried red chilies in a pan over low heat until fragrant (about 2-3 minutes).
  2. Grind the roasted spices into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. Set aside.
Step 2: Marinate the Chicken
  1. Clean the chicken pieces and pat them dry.
  2. In a bowl, mix the chicken with turmeric powder, chili powder, roasted curry powder, and the freshly ground spice paste (if using). Add a pinch of salt and mix well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for deeper flavor).
Step 3: Cook the Curry
  1. Heat oil in a large pot or pan over medium heat.
  2. Add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds (if using). Let them splutter.
  3. Add the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
  4. Add the chopped onions, green chilies, garlic, ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown and fragrant.
Step 4: Brown the Chicken
  1. Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pot. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is lightly browned on all sides.
Step 5: Simmer the Curry
  1. Pour in the thin coconut milk (or water) and stir well. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  2. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
Step 6: Add Thick Coconut Milk
  1. Once the chicken is cooked, add the thick coconut milk. Stir well and simmer for another 5-7 minutes on low heat. Do not boil vigorously after adding the thick coconut milk, as it may curdle.
  2. Taste and adjust salt and spice levels if needed. Add lime or lemon juice for a tangy kick (optional).
Step 7: Finish and Serve
  1. Remove the curry from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  2. Garnish with fresh curry leaves if desired.

Tips for the Best Sri Lankan Chicken Curry:

  1. Use Bone-In Chicken: Bone-in chicken adds more flavor to the curry.
  2. Fresh Coconut Milk: Freshly extracted coconut milk gives the curry a rich, creamy texture. If using canned coconut milk, mix it well before adding.
  3. Roasted Curry Powder: Sri Lankan roasted curry powder is key to the authentic flavor. If unavailable, use a good-quality curry powder.
  4. Adjust Spice Levels: Control the heat by adjusting the amount of green chilies, chili powder, and dried red chilies.
  5. Low and Slow Cooking: Simmer the curry on low heat to allow the flavors to develop fully.
