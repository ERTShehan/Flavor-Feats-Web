Sri Lankan Kottu

Kottu is a flavorful, stir-fried dish made with shredded roti (flatbread), vegetables, eggs, meat (or seafood), and a blend of aromatic spices. The rhythmic chopping and clanging of metal blades on a hot griddle are the signature sounds of kottu being prepared, making it a unique culinary experience. Here’s how you can make authentic Sri Lankan Kottu at home:

Ingredients:

For the Kottu:

  • 4-5 roti (store-bought or homemade), shredded into small pieces
  • 250g chicken, beef, or mutton (or seafood like shrimp), thinly sliced (optional)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned or grated
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 cup leeks or spring onions, chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons Sri Lankan roasted curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for spice)
  • 2-3 tablespoons soy sauce (optional, for added flavor)
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

For the Sauce (Optional but Recommended):

  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon tomato sauce or ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Roti

  1. If using store-bought roti, shred it into small, bite-sized pieces using a knife or scissors. If making homemade roti, cook and shred it similarly.

Step 2: Cook the Meat (Optional)

  1. In a large pan or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced chicken, beef, or mutton and cook until browned and cooked through. Season with salt, turmeric powder, and a pinch of roasted curry powder. Remove and set aside.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

  1. In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil.
  2. Add the chopped onions, garlic, ginger, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the onions are soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the carrots, cabbage, leeks, and tomatoes. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

Step 4: Add the Eggs

  1. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs into the other side.
  2. Scramble the eggs until cooked, then mix them with the vegetables.

Step 5: Combine Everything

  1. Add the shredded roti and cooked meat (if using) to the pan.
  2. Sprinkle turmeric powder, roasted curry powder, chili powder (if using), and salt. Mix well to coat everything evenly.
  3. If using the optional sauce, mix the coconut milk, tomato sauce, chili sauce, and soy sauce in a bowl. Pour it over the kottu and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.

Step 6: Finish and Serve

  1. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with lime wedges on the side.

Tips for Perfect Kottu:

  1. Use Day-Old Roti: Slightly stale roti works best as it holds up better during stir-frying.
  2. Customize the Protein: You can use chicken, beef, mutton, shrimp, or even tofu for a vegetarian version.
  3. Add More Vegetables: Feel free to add bell peppers, beans, or zucchini for extra nutrition.
  4. Adjust Spice Levels: Control the heat by adjusting the amount of green chilies and chili powder.
  5. Make It Saucy: The optional sauce adds moisture and flavor, but you can skip it if you prefer a drier kottu.
