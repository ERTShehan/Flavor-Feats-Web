String Hoppers (Idiyappam)

Here’s how you can make Sri Lankan String Hoppers with Coconut Sambal:

Ingredients:

For String Hoppers:
  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup boiling water (you may need a little more, depending on the consistency)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp coconut oil (optional for flavor)
For Coconut Sambal:
  • 1 ½ cups freshly grated coconut (or desiccated coconut if fresh isn’t available)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice level)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (optional, for extra spice)
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tbsp lime juice (or tamarind juice for tanginess)
  • Salt to taste
  • A handful of curry leaves (optional but adds great flavor)
  • 1-2 tbsp coconut oil (for tempering)

Instructions:

  1. Make the String Hoppers:
    1. Boil the water: Start by boiling water. In a mixing bowl, add the rice flour and salt. Gradually pour the boiling water over the rice flour, stirring continuously to form a dough. The dough should be smooth and pliable. If it's too dry, add a little more water, one tablespoon at a time.
    2. Shape the String Hoppers: Use a string hopper maker (or idiyappam maker) to press the dough into thin noodles. Arrange the noodles on the steaming tray in a circular pattern.
    3. Steam the String Hoppers: Fill a steamer with water, bring it to a boil, then place the string hopper tray inside. Cover and steam for about 8-10 minutes, or until the string hoppers are cooked through. Once cooked, remove from the steamer and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Coconut Sambal:
    1. Prepare the tempering: In a small pan, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds, and once they start popping, add the chopped onions and curry leaves (if using). Sauté until the onions become soft and translucent.
    2. Add spices and chilies: Add the chopped green chilies, red chili powder (if using), cumin powder, and a pinch of salt. Stir everything for 1-2 minutes.
    3. Mix in the grated coconut: Add the freshly grated coconut to the pan and mix well. Stir fry for 2-3 minutes, ensuring the coconut is coated with the spices.
    4. Finish with lime juice: Remove the pan from the heat and squeeze in lime juice (or tamarind juice for extra tang). Mix well.
  3. Serve:
    1. Serve the freshly steamed string hoppers with a generous portion of the coconut sambal on top or on the side.
    2. This dish is traditionally enjoyed with Sri Lankan dhal curry or a spicy curry, but the coconut sambal makes it delicious on its own.

Tips:

  • You can find string hopper makers online or at Sri Lankan or Indian grocery stores, and they make the process much easier.
  • If you're using desiccated coconut, hydrate it by soaking it in warm water for a few minutes to achieve the fresh coconut texture.
  • For a milder version, reduce the number of chilies or omit the chili powder.
  • You can also add a small amount of sugar to the sambal if you want a hint of sweetness to balance the spiciness.
