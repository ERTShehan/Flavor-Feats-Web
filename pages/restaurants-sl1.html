Sri Lankan Chicken Rice

Chicken Rice is a comforting meal that’s easy to prepare and perfect for family dinners or special occasions. The dish is infused with Sri Lankan flavors like curry leaves, pandan leaves (rampe), and roasted curry powder, making it a standout dish. Here’s how to make authentic Sri Lankan Chicken Rice:

Ingredients:

For the Chicken and Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (or any long-grain rice)
  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless, cut into pieces)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 pandan leaf (rampe, optional but adds a unique aroma)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (about 2 inches)
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons Sri Lankan roasted curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for spice)
  • 4 cups water (or chicken stock for extra flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Fried onions (optional, for extra crunch)

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Rice

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 20-30 minutes, then drain and set aside.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

  1. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with turmeric powder, roasted curry powder, chili powder (if using), and a pinch of salt. Let it marinate for at least 20 minutes.

Step 3: Cook the Chicken and Rice

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and cloves. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped onions, green chilies, garlic, ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  5. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is lightly browned.
  6. Add the drained rice to the pot and stir well to coat the rice with the spices and chicken.

Step 4: Simmer the Rice

  1. Pour in the water (or chicken stock) and add the pandan leaf (if using). Stir gently to combine.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

Step 5: Rest and Fluff the Rice

  1. Turn off the heat and let the rice rest, covered, for 5-10 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the rice to become fluffy.
  2. Gently fluff the rice with a fork to separate the grains.

Step 6: Serve

  1. Transfer the chicken rice to a serving platter.
  2. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves and fried onions (if using).
  3. Serve hot with a side of cucumber salad, papadum, or a spicy sambol.
