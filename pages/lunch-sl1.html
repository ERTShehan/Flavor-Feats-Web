Gotukola Sambol

Gotu Kola Sambol is a vibrant, refreshing, and nutritious Sri Lankan condiment that packs a punch of flavor and health benefits. It is made primarily with gotu kola leaves (Centella asiatica), a herb widely used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine. These leaves are known for their slightly bitter, earthy taste and are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. The sambol is a perfect balance of fresh, spicy, tangy, and savory flavors, making it a versatile accompaniment to rice, curry, or even bread.

What Makes Gotu Kola Sambol Special?

  • Freshness: The dish relies on fresh ingredients, especially the gotu kola leaves, which are finely chopped to release their flavor and nutrients.
  • Texture: The combination of crunchy onions, tender gotu kola leaves, and the soft, creamy texture of grated coconut creates a delightful mouthfeel.
  • Flavor Profile: The sambol is a harmony of flavorsâ€”spicy from green chilies, tangy from lime or lemon juice, and savory from the coconut and onions. The optional garlic adds a subtle depth of flavor.
  • Health Benefits: Gotu kola is revered for its medicinal properties, including improving circulation, boosting brain function, and promoting skin health. This sambol is not just delicious but also a powerhouse of nutrition.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups finely chopped gotu kola leaves (washed and cleaned)
  • 1 small red onion or shallot, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lime or lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated coconut (or desiccated coconut)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for extra spice)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Gotu Kola Leaves:
    • Wash the gotu kola leaves thoroughly under running water to remove any dirt or debris.
    • Pat them dry and finely chop the leaves. You can also bruise the leaves slightly with a mortar and pestle to release their flavor.

  2. Combine the Ingredients:
    • In a large mixing bowl, add the chopped gotu kola leaves, finely chopped red onion, green chilies, and minced garlic (if using).
    • Add the freshly grated coconut and mix everything well.

  3. Season the Sambol:
    • Drizzle the lime or lemon juice over the mixture. This not only adds tanginess but also helps prevent the gotu kola from oxidizing and turning brown.
    • Sprinkle salt, black pepper, and chili powder (if using). Mix thoroughly to ensure all the ingredients are evenly coated.

  4. Taste and Adjust:
    • Taste the sambol and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more lime juice for tanginess, salt for savoriness, or chilies for heat.

  5. Serve:
    • Transfer the sambol to a serving bowl. It is best served fresh to retain its vibrant flavors and textures.
    • Pair it with steamed rice, curry, roti, or even as a topping for toast.

Tips:

  • For a more authentic flavor, use freshly grated coconut instead of desiccated coconut.
  • Adjust the amount of green chilies and chili powder according to your spice preference.
  • Gotu Kola Sambol is best served fresh but can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a day.

Enjoy your homemade Sri Lankan Gotu Kola Sambol!
