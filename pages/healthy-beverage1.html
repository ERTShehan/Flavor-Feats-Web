Kurakkan Kanda(Finger Millet Porridge)

Kurakkan Kanda(Finger Millet Porridge) is a nutritious, gluten-free porridge that’s often enjoyed for breakfast or as a healthy snack. Kurakkan is rich in fiber, calcium, and iron, making this porridge a powerhouse of nutrition. It has a unique, slightly nutty flavor and a thick, creamy texture. Here's how to make authentic Sri Lankan Kurakkan Kanda:

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup kurakkan flour (finger millet flour)
  • 2 cups water (or coconut milk for a richer version)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1-2 tablespoons jaggery or sugar (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1/4 cup thick coconut milk (optional, for creaminess)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional, for flavor)
  • 1-2 tablespoons roasted cashews or almonds (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Kurakkan Flour Mixture

  1. In a small bowl, mix the kurakkan flour with 1/2 cup of water to form a smooth paste. Make sure there are no lumps.

Step 2: Cook the Porridge

  1. In a saucepan, bring the remaining 1.5 cups of water (or coconut milk) to a gentle boil over medium heat.
  2. Slowly add the kurakkan flour paste to the boiling water, stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and continue stirring until the mixture thickens into a smooth porridge (about 5-7 minutes).

Step 3: Season the Porridge

  1. Add salt and jaggery or sugar (if using) to the porridge. Stir well to combine.
  2. If you’re using cardamom powder, add it at this stage for a fragrant flavor.

Step 4: Finish the Porridge

  1. For a creamier texture, stir in 1/4 cup of thick coconut milk at the end of cooking. Mix well and remove from heat.

Step 5: Serve

  1. Pour the porridge into serving bowls.
  2. Garnish with roasted cashews or almonds for added crunch and flavor.
  3. Serve warm as a healthy breakfast or snack.
