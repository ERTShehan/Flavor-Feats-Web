Vegetable Chicken Soup

This soup is light yet satisfying, with a hint of Sri Lankan flavors from curry leaves, pandan leaves (rampe), and spices. It’s perfect for cold days, when you’re feeling under the weather, or simply when you crave a wholesome, homemade soup. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

For the Soup:

  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless, cut into pieces)
  • 1 liter water (or chicken stock for extra flavor)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (optional, for mild heat)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 pandan leaf (rampe, optional but adds a unique aroma)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (about 2 inches)
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for spice)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lime or lemon juice (optional, for tanginess)

For the Vegetables:

  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup leeks or celery, chopped (optional)
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded (optional)
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin or sweet potato, diced (optional)

For Garnish:

  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken Broth

  1. In a large pot, add the chicken pieces, water (or chicken stock), onion, garlic, ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, pandan leaf, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, black peppercorns, turmeric powder, and chili powder (if using).
  2. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  3. Skim off any impurities or foam that rise to the surface during cooking.

Step 2: Add the Vegetables

  1. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside to cool slightly.
  2. Add the diced carrots, potatoes, green beans, and any other vegetables you’re using to the pot. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but not mushy.

Step 3: Shred the Chicken

  1. While the vegetables are cooking, shred the cooked chicken into bite-sized pieces, discarding any bones if using bone-in chicken.
  2. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and stir to combine.

Step 4: Season the Soup

  1. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  2. If you prefer a tangy flavor, add a tablespoon of lime or lemon juice.

Step 5: Finish and Serve

  1. Remove the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and pandan leaf from the soup.
  2. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  3. Serve hot with a slice of lime or lemon on the side.

Serving Suggestions:

  • With Bread: Serve with crusty bread, roti, or pol roti for a hearty meal.
  • With Rice: Add a scoop of steamed rice to the soup for a more filling dish.
  • As a Light Meal: Enjoy the soup on its own as a light and nutritious meal.
